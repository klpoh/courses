Course Descriptions

dss5202 (4 units). This course equips students with the skills and knowledge to analyze and assess system sustainability using various tools such as life cycle analysis, input-output analysis, and multi-criteria decision analysis. It covers the evaluation of environmental impacts, effectiveness of sustainability policies and technologies, and designing sustainable solutions in areas like energy, transportation, agriculture, and waste management. Students will learn to conduct environmental impact assessments, identify sustainability risks, and develop practical solutions considering stakeholder perspectives. The course emphasizes practical applications and real-world case studies to provide hands-on experience in sustainability analysis and evaluation.

Course Outline

  1. Introduction to Systems Analysis
  2. Life Cycle Assessment
  3. Environmental Life Cycle Costing
  4. MCDM I: Analytic Hierarchy Process
  5. MCDM II: Multi-Objective Optimization of Sustainable Systems
  6. Sustainabiity Performance Evaluation using Data Envelopment Analysis
  7. Environmental Input-Output Analysis

Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of this course you will be able to:

  1. Perform life cycle assessment (LCA) of products and services.
  2. Perform environmental life cycle costings (LCC).
  3. Apply the Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) to make informed sustainable decisions.
  4. Apply multi-objective optimization (MOP) methods and understand trade-offs between sustainability, costs and other concerns.
  5. Perform sustainabilty input-output analysis.
  6. Perform integrated analysis on sustainabilty problems that include combination of some of the above methods.
