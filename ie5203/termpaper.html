Objectives

To provide the opportunity for students to apply the analytical knowledge, practical skills and software tools learned in this course to a selected real-world problem to help decision-makers and stakeholders make clear and defensible decisions

 

PhD/MEng students or those who aspire to pursue a research degree are welcome to do a research-oriented paper related to decision analysis instead of an application paper. Please consult the professor on relevant topics.

The Proposal

The one-page proposal should have the following information:

  • Your name, matriculation number and contact.
  • Title of term paper (tentative, but be specific as possible).
  • Problem background, description and objectives.
  • Decisions to be made.
  • Possible alternatives for each decision above.
  • Major factors and uncertainties.
  • Sources of data/information.
  • Software: DPL, YAAHP, Excel, Py, etc.
  • Other information.

The Final Report

The final report is due at the end of the reading week.

Suggested Contents

  • Cover Page
  • Executive Summary
  • Table of Contents
  1. Problem Description
  2. Base Model Formulation
  3. Deterministic Structuring
  4. Probabilistic Evaluation
  5. What-If, Scenario & Options Analysis
  6. Qualitative factors trade-off analysis (if any)
  7. Recommendations & Conclusions
  • References (if any, use APA format)
  • Appendix A (if any)
  • Appendix B (if any)
  • ...

Project Deliverables

  • Term Paper Report (PDF or Word)
  • Decision models (DPL, YAAHP etc) and other supporting computer code
  • ZIP all your files into ONE file, name it <Your Name>.zip
